sorting words and internationalization

Posted on September 11, 2016

Sorting words … who knew things would get hairy when you realize that ascii/utf encodings favors the English language when it comes to sorting .. so normal lexiographic based sorting will not work for other languages.

An example of such a problem?

 (sort ["beta" "beeg" "alpha","zelda","écrit"])

the result comes out to be: ("alpha" "beeg" "beta" "zelda" "écrit")

Lexiographic Sorting

Lexiographic sorting, if someone has to ask me what it is, the best explanations I can them is the first simple one:

Sorting words to be ordered to be as they would be in a dictionary

Stupid but it works. Stupid because the ordering in a dictionary is based off lexiographic ordering. I have explained the concept by giving an everyday example of the result of such sorting.

The second way would be

An Empty List of Words is already lexiographically sorted.
A list of one word is already lexiographically sorted.
Divide and order a list of words based off their first letter into sub-lists. Subdivide and order the words within the sub-lsists
into sub-sub-lists based off their second-letter, and continue the same till you reach the last letter of the longest word in the each of the resultant sub-sub-...-lists, and then flatten the lists. Hurrah!!!!

this is still stupid, as I doubt anyone would follow and also this describes the method, but not what lexiographic sorting is.

Well hopefully the combination of the ways of complaining the concept magically rings a metaphorical bell in your mind.
